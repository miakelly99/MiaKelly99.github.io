Biography

I recently received my M.S. from the University of Michigan. For the last two years, I studied the use of formal methods for verifying distributed protocols with Dr. Manos Kapritsos and GLaDOS@Michigan. Before beginning my Ph.D. I received my B.S. in Computer Science from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute. There, I was a member of the open source development team for Submitty, an open source grading platform, as well as being an experienced teaching assistant and tutor for both individual students and review sessions of over 150 students. At RPI I also served as the Vice-President and President of Upsilon Pi Epsilon, RPI's computer science honors society. I am the recipient of the RPI Founder's Award of Excellence and the RPI 4.0 Award.
